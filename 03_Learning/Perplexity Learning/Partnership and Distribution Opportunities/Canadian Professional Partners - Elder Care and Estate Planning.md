## Canadian Professionals Creating Content on Digital Organization and Document Management in Elder Care, Estate Planning, and Financial Advisory (2024-2025)

This research identifies Canadian professionals actively blogging, posting on LinkedIn, and creating educational content specifically about digital organization and document management as it relates to elder care, estate planning, and financial advisory services.

### **Estate Planning Lawyers and Firms**

**Major Firms with Active Content:**

**Miller Thomson LLP** has emerged as a leading content creator in the estate planning space. Their Private Client Services Group, led by Nicole Woodward (National Practice Group Leader), has published significant pieces including "The Digital Draft Dilemma: Is Your Estate Plan Vulnerable to a Single Unsigned Draft?" (November 2025), which directly addresses digital document management challenges in Ontario estate law. Woodward maintains a LinkedIn presence and presents regularly at professional conferences.[^1_1]

**Kimel Law Group** (Toronto) runs an active blog with Katherine Kimel as founder. Their piece "Managing Digital Assets: Protecting Your Digital Legacy" provides comprehensive guidance on incorporating digital assets into estate planning, including detailed instructions on document organization and access management.[^1_2]

**Hull \& Hull** has published multiple pieces on digital assets and estate planning, including "Jurisdictional Conflicts and Digital Assets" (September 2025) and earlier work "Navigating the Intersection of Digital Assets and Estates in Canada." This firm takes an international perspective on how different jurisdictions handle digital asset succession.[^1_3][^1_4]

**Bader Law** (Mississauga/Oakville) maintains an active blog with recent publications including "Secondary Wills in Ontario: Protecting Business Assets" (November 2025) and "Family Business Succession: Legal Challenges and Planning Strategies" (June 2025). These pieces address how to organize and manage complex estate documents.[^1_5][^1_6]

**Other Active Law Firms:**

- **Eisen Law**: "The Evolving Landscape of Digital Asset Succession" (October 2024)[^1_7]
- **Nihan Law**: "Digital Assets in a Will: What Happens to Your Online Life?" (July 2025)[^1_8]
- **J. Solomon Law**: "Digital Estate Planning: How to Protect Your Online Life" (September 2025)[^1_9]
- **AHBL**: "Your Digital Afterlife: Considerations for Estate Planning in the Cyberspace" (September 2025) and "Have You Planned For Your Digital Legacy?" (February 2025)[^1_10][^1_11]
- **Fogler, Rubinoff LLP**: Kathryn Balter (current Chair of OBA Trusts and Estates Law Section 2025-2026) is a frequent contributor to estate law publications and speaks regularly on wills and document management
- **Aird \& Berlis LLP**: Demetre Vasilounis (recently promoted to Partner in February 2025) specializes in digital asset management and succession planning. He maintains an active LinkedIn presence with posts on digital estate planning[^1_12][^1_13]

### **Emerging Trends in Content Coverage (2024-2025)**

The professionals and organizations identified are increasingly addressing:

1. **Digital Asset Management**: Cryptocurrency, online accounts, social media profiles, and cloud storage have become focal points for estate planning discussions[^1_32][^1_1][^1_3][^1_2][^1_8][^1_9]
2. **Document Organization**: Multiple resources now emphasize creating comprehensive inventories of physical and digital assets before crises occur[^1_2][^1_22][^1_26]
3. **Technology and Compliance**: Discussion of PIPEDA compliance, 24-hour breach reporting requirements, and privacy law implications for document storage[^1_23]
4. **Multi-Generational Planning**: Advisors and lawyers are helping clients prepare documentation that will ease transitions for family members and executors[^1_22][^1_16]
5. **Capacity and Powers of Attorney**: Increased focus on proper documentation of decision-making authority[^1_33][^1_31]
6. **Holistic Planning**: Financial advisors and elder care providers are addressing the emotional and relational aspects alongside financial and legal documentation[^1_20][^1_16]
