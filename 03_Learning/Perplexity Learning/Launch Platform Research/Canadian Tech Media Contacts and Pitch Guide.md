# What Canadian tech newsletters, podcasts, or publications have featured productivity or family tech products in 2024-2025? Who are the editors and how do they prefer pitches?

## Canadian Tech Newsletters, Podcasts, and Publications Featuring Productivity or Family Tech Products (2024-2025)

### **Major Publications \& News Outlets**

**BetaKit**[^1_1]
The publication of record for Canadian tech and startup news. Editor-in-Chief Douglas Soltys founded the independent version in 2016 and continues to lead it. BetaKit launched "Most Ambitious," an annual issue spotlighting innovation across Canada, and has published productivity-related coverage.

*Pitch Guidelines:* Soltys emphasizes that founders should pitch stories with compelling narratives and demonstrate understanding of what BetaKit covers. Direct pitches through their contact form on BetaKit.com or individual journalist bios work best. Focus on actual news rather than company announcements.[^1_2]

**The Logic**[^1_3][^1_4]
A subscription-based innovation economy news publication founded in 2018 by David Skok (CEO and Editor-in-Chief). Headquartered in Toronto with bureaus in Vancouver, Calgary, Ottawa, and Montreal, The Logic covers businesses, people, and policies driving transformational change. Their subscription model (\$299/year) ensures editorial independence.

*Pitch Preferences:* Email pitches should target specific reporters and demonstrate familiarity with The Logic's coverage style. They focus on innovation economy stories rather than straight product launches.[^1_3]

**TechDay Canada / ChannelLife Canada**[^1_5][^1_6][^1_7]
An independent technology publisher with operations in Toronto. Team includes Jake MacAndrew (Editor), Melania Watson (Interview Editor), and Shannon Williams (Journalist).

*Pitch Process:* Press releases should be sent to editors@techday.com. General inquiries go to hello@techday.com. Phone: +1 437 887 9190. They welcome story pitches and interview opportunities.[^1_6]

**MobileSyrup**[^1_8][^1_9]
An independent Canadian resource on mobile technology covering news, reviews, and carrier updates. Amanda Wawryk serves as Director of Editorial overseeing MobileSyrup alongside Daily Hive, blogTO, and Curiocity for the Zoomer Digital Network.

*Pitch Tips:* Amanda Wawryk advises PR professionals to research the organization thoroughly and tailor pitches accordingly. Avoid mass pitching; craft focused, relevant story ideas.[^1_10]

**WhatsYourTech.ca**[^1_11][^1_12][^1_13]
Founded by Yasmin Ranade (Publisher) as a collection of technology blogs by Canadians. Contributors include Christine Persaud, Gadjo Sevilla, Lee Rickwood, and Ted Kritsonis. Focus on consumer tech, mobile computing, cloud, and AI.

*Contact:* Yasmin@whatsyourtech.ca. The site welcomes guest posts and reader engagement on tech topics affecting Canadian consumers.[^1_12]

**WiFi HiFi Magazine**[^1_14][^1_15][^1_16]
Canada's trade publication for the consumer technology industry. Editors include Christine Persaud (Editor-in-Chief, cpersaud@wifihifi.ca) and John Thomson (jthomson@wifihifi.ca). Produces print magazines, weekly newsletters, and daily web content covering consumer tech from a Canadian perspective.

*Coverage:* Has featured smart home technology, family tech products, and productivity tools. Pitch directly to Christine Persaud or John Thomson via email.[^1_16][^1_14]

**Daily Hive**[^1_9][^1_17][^1_10]
Multi-market digital media outlet with Amanda Wawryk as Director of Editorial. Darcy Matheson is Editor-in-Chief. Has city-specific editions covering tech, business, and local innovation.

*Pitch Strategy:* Research the specific city edition you're targeting. Direct pitches showing familiarity with their coverage style perform best.[^1_10]

**GetConnected Radio \& Podcast**[^1_18]
Canada's top technology show airing live across Canada on the Corus Radio Network every Saturday. Hosted by Mike Agerbo, John Biehler, and expert guests. Covers new technology topics, Canadian innovation, and tech literacy.

*Submission:* Subscribe to the GetConnected podcast on major platforms. Show aired live in Vancouver on CKNW at 10:00am PST Saturdays.

**The App Show Radio \& Podcast**[^1_18]
Exclusively about apps and software for daily life. Hosted by Mike Agerbo, John Biehler, Graham Williams, and expert guests. Covers both Android and iOS platforms.

*Air Details:* Airs Sundays at 10:00am PST on CKNW in Vancouver with episodes available on podcast platforms.

**Tech Talk with Marc Saltzman**[^1_19][^1_20][^1_21]
Syndicated weekly radio show hosted by prolific tech expert Marc Saltzman. Airs on Toronto's NewsTalk 1010 (Bell Media), Montreal's CJAD 800 (Bell Media), and Winnipeg's 680 CJOB (Corus), plus 248+ U.S. stations via Radio America network.

*Coverage:* Covers consumer electronics, productivity apps, smart home solutions, family tech, and business tech. Marc has 25+ years of experience and contributes to 30+ publications including USA Today, Yahoo, and MSN.

*Contact:* marc@marcsaltzman.com or visit marcsaltzman.com. Marc frequently appears on CP24, CTV, and Global TV with tech segments.[^1_22][^1_23][^1_19]

**A Productive Conversation Podcast**[^1_24]
Hosted by productivity strategist Mike Vardy. Features discussions on time devotion, productiveness, and refining approaches to daily living, with guests who are thinkers and creators.[^1_24]

**The Daily Mastery Podcast by Robin Sharma**[^1_24]
Toronto-based podcast hosted by Robin Sharma covering mental models, daily routines, and productivity tactics. 4.9/5 rating on Apple Podcasts with 500+ reviews.

**CP24/CTV News Toronto**[^1_30][^1_31][^1_22]
Jamie Gutfreund (CP24 News Anchor \& Reporter) and other correspondents cover tech segments. Coverage includes smart home products, family tech, and productivity solutions. Contact: jamie.gutfreund@ctv.ca.

*Segments:* Regular tech features on CP24 Breakfast and newsflow covering consumer technology relevant to Greater Toronto Area audiences.[^1_30][^1_22]

### **Pitch Preferences - Key Takeaways**

**What Canadian Tech Journalists Want:**[^1_32][^1_2]

- Actual news and story angles, not company announcements
- Tailored pitches demonstrating familiarity with their coverage
- Clear, concise emails without marketing jargon
- Early notification (5-7 business days advance for timely pieces)
- Direct contact through published channels (forms, individual journalist emails)
- Stories about founders' journeys, policy impacts, and innovation driving change
- Exclusive information or unique perspectives when possible

**Worst Practices to Avoid:**

- Generic mass pitches
- Starting emails with "I have a pitch for you"
- Pitching topics without a specific news angle
- Contacting multiple editors simultaneously
- Failing to research the publication's previous coverage

Most Canadian tech media outlets prefer email pitches sent to specific editors during business hours. Doing your homework on what each publication covers will significantly increase your chances of coverage.
